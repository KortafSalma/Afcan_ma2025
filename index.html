About AFCON 2025 Morocco

The 35th edition of the Africa Cup of Nations returns to Morocco after decades of anticipation. This tournament promises to be the most spectacular edition yet, featuring state-of-the-art stadiums, passionate fans, and the best football talent Africa has to offer.

Morocco, with its rich football history and modern infrastructure, is ready to host an unforgettable celebration of African football that will showcase the continent's unity, talent, and passion for the beautiful game.

TOURNAMENT STADIUMS

Complexe Sportif Prince Moulay Abdellah

City: Rabat

Capacity: 53,000

Status: Tournament Final

Built: 1983 (Renovated 2024)

Recently renovated for AFCON 2025 with modern facilities, VIP sections, and improved accessibility. Will host the tournament final and several knockout stage matches.

Stade Mohammed V

City: Casablanca

Capacity: 67,000

Status: Semi-Finals

Built: 1955 (Renovated 2023)

Historic stadium with electric atmosphere. Renovated with new seating, lighting, and media facilities for AFCON 2025. Will host key matches including quarter-finals and semi-finals.

Grand Stade de Marrakech

City: Marrakech

Capacity: 45,240

Status: Group Stage & Knockout

Built: 2011

Modern stadium built in 2011. Features unique architecture inspired by Moroccan design. Known for excellent pitch quality and fan amenities.

Grand Stade de Tanger

City: Tangier

Capacity: 65,000

Status: Quarter-Finals

Built: 2011

Also known as Stade Ibn Batouta. New stadium with stunning sea views of Tangier Bay. One of Africa's most modern football venues with excellent facilities and accessibility.

Stade Adrar

City: Agadir

Capacity: 45,480

Status: Group Stage

Built: 2013 (Renovated 2024)

Renovated for AFCON 2025 with improved facilities and seating. Known for its excellent pitch quality and modern design inspired by traditional Moroccan architecture.

Complexe de Fès

City: Fes

Capacity: 40,000

Status: Group Stage

Built: 2007 (Renovated 2024)

Supporting venue for group stage matches. Recently renovated with improved spectator facilities. Known for its passionate local supporters and historic city backdrop.

HOST CITIES & FAMOUS LANDMARKS

Rabat

Capital City & Administrative Center

Famous Landmark: Hassan Tower & Mausoleum

Location: Central Rabat

The Hassan Tower is the minaret of an incomplete mosque from the 12th century. The nearby Mausoleum of Mohammed V contains the tombs of Moroccan kings.

Casablanca

Economic Capital & Largest City

Famous Landmark: Hassan II Mosque

Location: Casablanca coast

The Hassan II Mosque is the largest mosque in Africa and the 7th largest in the world. Its minaret is the world's tallest at 210 meters (689 ft).

Marrakech

Cultural Capital & Tourist Hub

Famous Landmark: Jemaa el-Fnaa Square

Location: Marrakech Medina

Jemaa el-Fnaa is the main square and market place in Marrakech's medina quarter. By day it's filled with orange juice stalls and snake charmers, by night with food stalls.

Tangier

Strategic Port City

Famous Landmark: Tangier Kasbah

Location: Old Medina of Tangier

The Kasbah of Tangier is a historic fortress in the old medina. It offers stunning views of the Strait of Gibraltar and houses the Kasbah Museum of Mediterranean Cultures.

Agadir

Coastal Resort City

Famous Landmark: Agadir Kasbah

Location: Hilltop overlooking Agadir

The Kasbah of Agadir is a historic fortification built in 1540. Destroyed in the 1960 earthquake, its ruins offer panoramic views of Agadir's bay and the modern city below.

Fes

Historic & Spiritual Capital

Famous Landmark: Fes el Bali Medina

Location: Fes Old City

Fes el Bali is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's largest urban pedestrian zones. It's home to the University of Al Quaraouiyine, founded in 859 and considered the oldest continuously operating university.
